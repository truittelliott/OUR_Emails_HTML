The Discovery Lab Research Experience


The Discovery Lab is a state-of-the-art hub for student-led STEM research, prototyping, and collaborative projects. Located on the first floor of the new Undergraduate Science Lab Building (USLB). The lab is outfitted with specialized instruments and equipment designed to spark creativity, exploration, and transformative problem solving. Projects include freshwater ecosystem analysis, fabricating environmental sensors, and microplastics detection. 

The deadline to apply is August 22, with applicants accepted on a rolling basis afterwards. For more information, including how to apply, see the Discovery Lab Research Experience outreach materials and application form.

Center for Applied Behavioral Systems Fall Applications


The Center for Applied Behavior Systems (CABS) was developed to help undergraduate and graduate students learn how to conduct research that combines the technology of applied behavioral science with theories from positive, social, and humanistic psychology; and give students real-world, hands-on research experience. 


Students interested in joining the center this Fall should submit their applications by August 25, 2025 at 5 P.M.  For more information, including how to apply, visit the CABS Fall 2025 application form.

Undergraduate Researchers for Smart Healthcare Hub


The "Building the Future of Technology Implementation in Healthcare: Human Systems Integration" (a.k.a. "Smart Healthcare Hub") initiative at Virginia Tech and VT Carilion is accepting applications from motivated undergraduate researchers from all backgrounds to join us during the Fall 2025 and Spring 2026 terms. Undergraduate student researchers will assist with planning, data collection, and analysis for empirical behavioral research related to clinical training simulations and AI applications in healthcare. Students Accepted students will receive up to 3 hours of undergraduate research or field study credit for their work during the term.
 
Students should apply on handshake. Questions can be directed to Dr. James D. Ivory (jivory@vt.edu).

Keesee Sustainability Grant


The University Libraries at Virginia Tech is pleased to invite undergraduate students in all disciplines and academic programs to apply through a competitive process to receive funding for sustainability research and projects. Potential topics include (but are not limited to): ecological sustainability, environmental sustainability, environmental conservation, policy and sustainability.


Applicants may request up to $2000. Funding may cover project expenses such as supplies and travel costs. Recipients will also become members of the Newman Library Project Design Studio. The deadline to apply is August 31. For more information, including how to apply, visit the Project Design Studio webpage.

Network for Undergraduate Research in Virginia (NURVa) Conference Registration


The NURVa conference is an opportunity for undergraduates in all disciplines to present their research and creative work at a statewide conference. The 6th annual conference will be held on Saturday, November 1, 2025, on the campus of the University of Mary Washington in Fredericksburg. Conference registration, which includes continental breakfast and lunch, is $25.
 
NURVa invites submissions from undergraduates in all disciplines in the categories of oral presentation, poster, or performance. Submit abstracts (750 words maximum) with one letter of support from a faculty mentor on the NURVa Conference Submission Form. The submission deadline is midnight, September 15, 2025.

Interdisciplinary Projects Course


The Interdisciplinary Projects (IDPro) program at Virginia Tech bridges classroom learning and real-world application through multi-semester, client-based projects. Students from various majors collaborate on industry, research, or service-learning projects, gaining invaluable professional skills and experiences. IDPro offers mentorship, resources, and industry engagement, empowering students to tackle real-world problems and develop as professionals.

For more information on the showcase, course, and how to enroll in the Fall, visit the IDPro course website.

Engineering Biology Course-based Research Experience - BSE 4014


The Synthetic Biology Lab course has several open spots this coming semester. This semester students will be using CRISPR to knockout, inhibit, or activate genes central to tryptophan and auxin metabolism in yeast and measure the effects of our CRISPR modulations with a fluorescent auxin biosensor. Students will learn modular cloning for rapid, flexible, combinatorial DNA assembly, Opentrons liquid handling lab automation, CRISPR knockout/activation/inhibition technology, as well as biosensor design principles, and basic metabolic engineering.
