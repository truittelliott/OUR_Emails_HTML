Roger and Debbie West Student Grant


The Institute for Creativity, Arts, and Technology (ICAT) invites applicants for projects that leverage the combined strengths of science, engineering, art, and design to tackle real-world challenges. Teams must be comprised of students from at least two different schools/department, and one team member must represent arts and/or design. ICAT will award five, $1,000 student grants.

The deadline to apply is October 6. For more information, including how to apply, visit the Roger and Debbie West Student Grant webpage.

The Biomedical Data Science in Context Program at The Jackson Laboratory


The Biomedical Data Science in Context program at The Jackson Laboratory in Bar Harbor, Maine, is an interdisciplinary experience designed for students who want to explore how data science can be applied to biological research. Participants will work with real biomedical datasets, gain hands-on experience in computational analysis, and engage in discussions around the ethical implications of data science. 

This two-week residential program is well-suited for students who are curious about integrating computational tools into their research or considering careers in biomedical research, genomics, or bioinformatics. All admitted students will receive full scholarships to cover student travel, as well as housing and meals during the program. The program will run from January 3-17. 

Applications are due October 15. For more information, including how to apply, visit the Biomedical Data Science in Context Program webpage.

Global Change Center Undergraduate Research Grants


Students mentored by Global Change Center (GCC) faculty are invited apply for undergraduate research grant. Projects that address basic and/or applied aspects of global change science, engineering, social science and the humanities are eligible for this funding opportunity, and proposals must include strong endorsement by a GCC-affiliated faculty member.

The deadline to apply is Friday, September 26. For more information, including how to apply, visit the GCC Undergraduate Research Grants webpage.

Radiance Technologies Innovation Bowl


The Radiance Technologies Innovation Bowl is an academic competition between schools and conferences affiliated with the Radiance Technologies Independence Bowl. Affiliated schools compete for a single $25,000 Grand Prize by developing innovative approaches to a current research and development topic selected by Radiance Technologies.

Teams must register by September 26, 2025. For more information, including how to register, visit the Radiance Technologies webpage.

ICTAS Undergraduate Research Fellowship


The Institute for Critical Technology and Applied Science (ICTAS) Undergraduate Research Fellowship Program supports early career undergraduates involved in mentored research at Virginia Tech. Each spring, one student will be selected to receive the $1,250 award, payable as a fellowship or used to cover other approved expenditures (such as project supplies, research-related travel, use of specialized research services, etc) over the course of one semester or a summer.

Applications are due November 10, 2025. More information on eligibility criteria and how to apply can be found on the ICTAS Undergraduate Research Fellowship Program webpage.

Biosafety and Biosecurity Month


To celebrate National Biosafety and Biosecurity Month, which is throughout the month of October, the institutional Biosafety Committee program will host an awareness event on October 6, in the interdisciplinary life sciences building from 9:00am until 4:00pm. Although focused on biosafety, the event is open to the university community. Attendees can meet the IBCP and EHS Biosafety group staff, collect swag, participate in activities (trivia, photo booth, etc.), sign up for raffle prizes, and participate in a new biosafety-themed escape room. 

For more information, visit the Institutional Biosafety Committee webpage. 

Microfluidic Patch-Pump Human Subject Recruitment Survey


The Fluid Dynamics in Nature Laboratory (FINLAB) in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Virginia Tech is recruiting participants to assist in the development of a wrist-worn, pulse-driven device for liquid drug delivery across the skin. In order to develop pumps that generate the correct delivery rate, we will test the performance of a wide array of pumps on participants. 

For more information on how to participate in the study, visit the recruitment survey.
