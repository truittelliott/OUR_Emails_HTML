MAOP Summer Research Programs
Interested in starting undergraduate research this summer? Consider joining the MAOP summer research programs - The MAOP Summer Undergraduate Research Internship, and Hoki Summer Scholars. 

These 10 week programs support students with on-campus housing, meal plan, and stipend. Both programs also give students individualized support and professional development opportunities.  

To be notified when applications open later this year, and to learn more about eligibility, please complete the MAOP Undergraduate Research Summer Programs interest form.

Roger and Debbie West Student Grant


The Institute for Creativity, Arts, and Technology (ICAT) invites applicants for projects that leverage the combined strengths of science, engineering, art, and design to tackle real-world challenges. Teams must be comprised of students from at least two different schools/department, and one team member must represent arts and/or design. ICAT will award five, $1,000 student grants.

The deadline to apply is October 6. For more information, including how to apply, visit the Roger and Debbie West Student Grant webpage.

Multicultural Academic Opportunities Program (MAOP) Summer Research Internship


The primary goal of this 10-week program is to help students explore career goals and grow professionally. Participants will benefit from:
  • Weekly research & professional development seminars
  • Occasional social events
  • A final research symposium with poster presentations
  • On-campus housing, meal plan, and $3,000 stipend
For more information, and to be notified when applications open, complete the MAOP Summer Research Internship interest form.

Network for Undergraduate Research in Virginia (NURVa) Conference Registration


The NURVa conference is an opportunity for undergraduates in all disciplines to present their research and creative work at a statewide conference. The 6th annual conference will be held on Saturday, November 1, 2025, on the campus of the University of Mary Washington in Fredericksburg. Conference registration, which includes continental breakfast and lunch, is $25.
 
NURVa invites submissions from undergraduates in all disciplines in the categories of oral presentation, poster, or performance. Submit abstracts (750 words maximum) with one letter of support from a faculty mentor on the NURVa Conference Submission Form. The submission deadline is midnight, September 15, 2025.

Global Change Center Undergraduate Research Grants


Students mentored by Global Change Center (GCC) faculty are invited apply for undergraduate research grant. Projects that address basic and/or applied aspects of global change science, engineering, social science and the humanities are eligible for this funding opportunity, and proposals must include strong endorsement by a GCC-affiliated faculty member.

The deadline to apply is Friday, September 26. For more information, including how to apply, visit the GCC Undergraduate Research Grants webpage.

Mid-Atlantic Regional Conference for Undergraduate Scholarship (MARCUS)


The 2025 Mid-Atlantic Regional Conference for Undergraduate Scholarship (MARCUS) is Saturday, November 8, 2025, at Randolph College. This conference accepts undergraduate works from all disciplines. Students who wish to present will have submitted the presentation application by the end of Wednesday, October 8, to be scheduled and included in the program

ICTAS Undergraduate Research Fellowship


The Institute for Critical Technology and Applied Science (ICTAS) Undergraduate Research Fellowship Program supports early career undergraduates involved in mentored research at Virginia Tech. Each spring, one student will be selected to receive the $1,250 award, payable as a fellowship or used to cover other approved expenditures (such as project supplies, research-related travel, use of specialized research services, etc) over the course of one semester or a summer.

Applications are due November 10, 2025. More information on eligibility criteria and how to apply can be found on the ICTAS Undergraduate Research Fellowship Program webpage.

Experiential Learning Opportunities at Homefield Farm


Homefield Farm is an organic 12-acre collaboration between Dining Services and the College of Agriculture and Life Sciences. Located at Kentland Farm, it is a location for produce production and experiential learning. 

If you're interested in learning more about plants, how our produce is processed, or simply getting outside, this is the perfect opportunity for you! Experience will include everything from planting, to harvesting, to processing.


For more information, including how to apply, go to the Homefield Farm application form. 

Majors & Minors Fair


With more than 100 different majors and a wide variety of minors offered at Virginia Tech, choosing the right academic path can be a difficult decision. The fair will be on Wednesday, October 1 from 10am - 3pm in Squires Student Center. For more information, visit the Majors & Minors Fair webpage.
