Technical University of Munich Practical Research Experience Program (TUM PREP)


Each Summer, the Technical University of Munich (TUM) invites excellent students from leading North American universities and the Imperial College London to spend an approx. 8-week long research stay at TUM. Participating students gain valuable insights into the research work at Germany’s top-ranked technical university and enhance their technical and methodological qualifications.

Students can apply between October 1 and November 23, 2025. For more information, visit the Technical University of Munich PREP webpage. 

Discovery Lab Research Experience - Spring Applications Open


The Discovery Lab is a state-of-the-art hub for student-led STEM research, prototyping, and collaborative projects. Located on the first floor of the new Undergraduate Science Lab Building (USLB). Projects include freshwater ecosystem analysis, fabricating environmental sensors, and microplastics detection.  

The deadline to apply is December 1, with applicants accepted on a rolling basis afterwards. For more information, including how to apply, see the Discovery Lab Research Experience outreach materials and application form.

Integrated Health Sciences & Research Experience


The IHSR experience offers a two- to three-year experiential learning opportunity for Virginia Tech undergraduate students of any major seeking extensive research involvement in various biomedical fields such as neuroscience, cancer, health behaviors, addiction and recovery, neurobiology, exercise medicine, and neuromotor research. Accepted students begin with a 1-credit course that introduces them to biomedical research before securing a placement in one of nearly 45 laboratories at the Fralin Biomedical Research Institute in Roanoke, Virginia.

Once matched with a lab, students will coordinate their academic schedules to allow for at least 10 hours of research over any two-day period each week, traveling by car or bus between Blacksburg and Roanoke. By graduation, many students will have the chance to present their research at local symposiums, with some even achieving publication alongside their faculty mentor.

The deadline to apply is January 31, 2026. For more information, and to apply, visit the Integrated Health Sciences and Research Experience webpage. 

Summer Pediatric Oncology Education (POE) Program


The POE program offers a unique opportunity for pre-doctoral students preparing for careers in the biomedical sciences, medicine, and pharmacy to gain biomedical and oncology research experience. Students participate in basic or clinical oncology research, a core lecture series designed specifically for them, as well as research and clinical conferences.

POE medical students spend a minimum of 10 weeks in the program. The minimum tenure requirement for all others is 11 weeks. Program participants will receive a $600/week subsistence allowance. Fully furnished group housing will be provided at no cost for non-local participants.

Applications are due February 1, 2026. For more information, and to apply, visit the Pediatric Oncology Education Program webpage. 

National Conference for Undergraduate Research (NCUR)


The National Conference on Undergraduate Research (NCUR) is dedicated to promoting undergraduate research, scholarship and creative activity in all fields of study by sponsoring an annual conference for students. Unlike meetings of academic professional organizations, this gathering of student scholars welcomes presenters from all institutions of higher learning and from all disciplines.

The conference will take place on April 13-15, 2026 in Richmond, VA. The deadline to submit an abstract is December 5, 2025. For more information, including how to register, visit the NCUR 2026 webpage.

Fall Research Integrity and Scholarly Excellence (RISE) Lecture Series


Science is facing unimagined challenges from many different directions. In the Fall Research Integrity and Scholarly Excellence (RISE) Lecture Series event, Josh Fessel, a physician-scientist, will provide a personal reflection and answer the question -- What do I do and what do we do? Earlier this year Fessel resigned from his position as the National Institutes of Health Chief Medical Officer due to his conflict with directives that he believed compromised scientific integrity. Learn how this experience shaped his perspective on the importance of scholarly integrity in higher education research. 
  • Friday, November 14 | Informal Q&A 9:30-10:30, Formal Presentation: 11 a.m. – 12 p.m. 
  • Location: Steger Hall Auditorium

Survey on Pathways to Graduate STEM Programs


We invite you to participate in a short survey as part of the Pathways to STEM Excellence project: a collaboration between Norfolk State University (NSU) and Virginia Tech (VT). This study is designed to support underrepresented minority (URM) students by identifying and addressing barriers to graduate education in STEM fields.

The survey takes ~15-20 minutes to complete. Qualified participants of the survey will obtain a $5 gift card to a local establishment. To complete the survey, visit the Survey on Pathways to Graduate STEM Programs webpage.
