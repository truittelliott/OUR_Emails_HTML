Undergraduate Research Showcase at the Capital

The Network for Undergraduate Research in Virginia (NURVa) is excited to announce the second Undergraduate Research Showcase at the Capitol on Thursday, January 29, 2026, from 3:00-5:00 p.m. at the General Assembly Building in Richmond, VA.

Virginia Tech will be nominating 5 student presenters to represent the quality and diversity of research taking place at VT. From our nominations, NURVa will select 3 presenters to represent Virginia Tech. All presentations will be in poster format. There is no charge to present at this event; VT will provide transportation.

The deadline to apply is Friday, November 14 at 5pm. For more information, including how to apply, visit the Undergraduate Research Showcase at the Capital application form.

Discovery Lab Research Experience - Spring Applications Open


The Discovery Lab is a state-of-the-art hub for student-led STEM research, prototyping, and collaborative projects. Located on the first floor of the new Undergraduate Science Lab Building (USLB). Integrated Health Sciences & Research Experience


The IHSR experience offers a two- to three-year experiential learning opportunity for Virginia Tech undergraduate students of any major seeking extensive research involvement in various biomedical fields such as neuroscience, cancer, health behaviors, addiction and recovery, neurobiology, exercise medicine, and neuromotor research. Accepted students begin with a 1-credit course that introduces them to biomedical research before securing a placement in one of nearly 45 laboratories at the Fralin Biomedical Research Institute in Roanoke, Virginia.

Once matched with a lab, students will coordinate their academic schedules to allow for at least 10 hours of research over any two-day period each week, traveling by car or bus between Blacksburg and Roanoke. By graduation, many students will have the chance to present their research at local symposiums, with some even achieving publication alongside their faculty mentor.

The deadline to apply is January 31, 2026. For more information, and to apply, visit the Integrated Health Sciences and Research Experience webpage. 

ICTAS Undergraduate Research Fellowship


The Institute for Critical Technology and Applied Science (ICTAS) Undergraduate Research Fellowship Program supports early career undergraduates involved in mentored research at Virginia Tech. Each spring, one student will be selected to receive the $1,250 award, payable as a fellowship or used to cover other approved expenditures (such as project supplies, research-related travel, use of specialized research services, etc) over the course of one semester or a summer.

Applications are due November 10, 2025. More information on eligibility criteria and how to apply can be found on the ICTAS Undergraduate Research Fellowship Program webpage.

National Conference for Undergraduate Research (NCUR)


The National Conference on Undergraduate Research (NCUR) is dedicated to promoting undergraduate research, scholarship and creative activity in all fields of study by sponsoring an annual conference for students. Unlike meetings of academic professional organizations, this gathering of student scholars welcomes presenters from all institutions of higher learning and from all disciplines.

The conference will take place on April 13-15, 2026 in Richmond, VA. The deadline to submit an abstract is December 5, 2025. For more information, including how to register, visit the NCUR 2026 webpage.

National Science Foundation - Graduate Research Fellowship Program


The National Science Foundation is seeking applicants for its Graduate Research Fellowship Program (GRFP). Since 1952, the GRFP has recognized and supported outstanding graduate students who are pursuing full-time research-based master's and doctoral degrees in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) fields, including STEM education. Selected students will recieve: 
  • Financial support for three years (within a five-year period) 
  • Stipend: $37,000 
  • Cost of Education allowance: $16,000 per year (paid to the institution covering tuition and mandatory fees) 
  • No service requirement after completion of the fellowship
The deadline to submit recommendations in November 7, 2025. For more information, including how to apply, visit the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program webpage.
